Step 1 — Define Your Product

Get clarity on what you're creating, who it's for, and what transformation it delivers.

Fill in your details
Generated prompt
I want to create an ebook to sell on Hotmart. Help me define my product based on the information below: - General topic: [YOUR TOPIC] - Target audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE] - Problem it solves: [THE PROBLEM YOU SOLVE] - Desired transformation: [WHAT THE READER ACHIEVES] - Desired format: ebook of approximately [NUMBER OF PAGES] Based on this, give me: 1. A clear one-sentence value proposition 2. A main title (impactful, results-oriented) 3. 3 alternative subtitle options 4. The product's core promise 5. 3 common objections the audience would have, and how to overcome each one

Step 2 — Create a Strategic Outline

Build the backbone of your ebook — a table of contents that tells a transformation story.

Fill in your details
Generated prompt
I need to create a table of contents for an ebook with the following details: - Title: [EBOOK TITLE] - Audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE] - Core promise: [WHAT THE READER WILL ACHIEVE] - Desired number of chapters: [NUMBER OF CHAPTERS] Create a complete outline that: 1. Starts with a chapter that identifies the problem (the reader must see themselves in it) 2. Progresses logically from foundational concepts to advanced application 3. Includes at least one 'quick wins' chapter with immediate results 4. Ends with an action plan / next-steps chapter 5. For each chapter: chapter title, 2–3 main subtopics, and the specific transformation it delivers
✦ Tip: Use this prompt once per chapter — fill in new details each time. Generating one chapter at a time produces significantly better quality than asking for all at once.

Step 3 — Write a Chapter

Reuse this prompt for every chapter in your outline.

Fill in your details
Generated prompt
Write Chapter [NUMBER] of my ebook "[EBOOK TITLE]". Chapter title: [CHAPTER TITLE] Subtopics to cover: [LIST YOUR SUBTOPICS] Audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE] Tone of voice: [YOUR PREFERRED TONE] Desired length: [WORD COUNT] Instructions: - Open with an emotional hook or a relatable situation the reader recognizes - Use concrete, real-world examples from everyday work life - Include at least 1 practical exercise or reflection question at the end - Write in natural, conversational language — avoid unnecessary jargon - Close with a smooth transition hinting at the next chapter - Include at least 1 relevant quote or data point if possible
✦ Tip: Write the introduction LAST — after all chapters are done. You'll know your content better and write a much stronger hook.

Step 4A — Write the Introduction

The most important section of your ebook — it's what keeps readers going.

Fill in your details
Generated prompt
Write the introduction for my ebook "[EBOOK TITLE]" using the AIDA structure (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action). Details: - Target audience: [YOUR AUDIENCE] - Main pain the reader feels: [DESCRIBE THE PAIN] - What the ebook delivers: [CORE PROMISE] - Tone of voice: [YOUR TONE] The introduction must: 1. Open with a story or real situation that makes the reader say "that's exactly me!" 2. Validate the reader's pain without judgment 3. Introduce the author's credibility in 2–3 natural sentences 4. Make a clear promise of what the reader will learn 5. Preview the chapters in an exciting, scannable way 6. Close with a call to action to keep reading Length: 400 to 600 words

Step 4B — Write the Conclusion

Close strong — celebrate the reader and give them a clear next action.

Fill in your details
Generated prompt
Write the conclusion for my ebook "[EBOOK TITLE]". The conclusion must: 1. Give an emotional (not technical) summary of the journey the reader just completed 2. Celebrate the reader for getting all the way through 3. Provide a '7-day action plan' with practical implementation steps 4. Include a clear call to action: [WHAT SHOULD THE READER DO NEXT] 5. Close with a memorable, encouraging final sentence Tone: warm and motivating Length: 300 to 500 words
⚡ This step is crucial: Your sales page copy is what converts visitors into buyers. It's the highest revenue-impact piece you'll write — invest real time here.
